TOPIC 2: THE MOLE CONCEPT

BASIC DEFINITIONS

mole
amount of substance containing 6.02 x 10^23 particles

Avogadro constant
6.02 x 10^23 particles per mole

Ar (relative atomic mass)
average mass of an atom compared to 1/12 of carbon-12
no unit

Mr (relative molecular mass)
sum of all Ar values in a molecule
no unit

molar mass
mass of 1 mole of a substance
unit: g/mol

dm^3 (cubic decimetre)
unit of volume
1 dm^3 = 1 litre = 1000 cm^3

rtp (room temperature and pressure)
conditions where 1 mole of gas occupies 24 dm^3

KEY FORMULAS

moles = mass / molar mass
mass = moles x molar mass

number of particles = moles x 6.02 x 10^23
moles = number of particles / 6.02 x 10^23

volume = moles x 24 (for gases at rtp)
moles = volume / 24

CONVERSIONS

mass <-> moles using molar mass
moles <-> particles using Avogadro constant
moles <-> volume using 24 dm^3 (rtp)

PERCENTAGE COMPOSITION

percentage = (mass of element in compound / Mr) x 100

EMPIRICAL FORMULA STEPS

assume total mass = 100 g
convert mass to moles
divide all by smallest value
simplify to whole number ratio

MOLECULAR FORMULA

molecular formula = empirical formula x n
n = Mr / empirical formula mass

TOPIC 3: CHEMICAL CALCULATIONS

BALANCED EQUATIONS

must balance before calculations
do not change formulas
only change coefficients

STOICHIOMETRY

based on mole ratios from balanced equation
mass is conserved

CORE METHOD

step 1 write balanced equation
step 2 convert given value to moles
step 3 use mole ratio from equation
step 4 convert to required unit

USING MOLAR MASS AND VOLUME

use molar mass for solids and liquids
use 24 dm^3 for gases at rtp

GAS VOLUME RATIOS

gas volumes follow mole ratios at same conditions

LIMITING REACTANT

reactant that runs out first
determines amount of product formed

steps:
calculate moles of all reactants
use mole ratio to compare
reactant producing less product is limiting

EXCESS REACTANT

reactant left over after reaction

PERCENTAGE YIELD

percentage yield = (actual yield / theoretical yield) x 100

theoretical yield = calculated maximum amount
actual yield = amount obtained from experiment

PERCENTAGE PURITY

percentage purity = (mass of pure substance / total mass) x 100

COMMON MISTAKES

not balancing equation
skipping mole conversion
wrong mole ratio
not using 24 dm^3 for gases
ignoring significant figures

FINAL FLOW

mass -> moles -> ratio -> answer
particles -> moles -> ratio -> answer
volume -> moles -> ratio -> answer
